# Risk, Compliance, and Disaster Recovery

## Risk management

Use a lightweight risk process for major systems and initiatives.

- Identify key technical and operational risks
- Assess likelihood, impact, and mitigation options
- Assign owner and review cadence
- Track mitigation progress and residual risk

## Security vulnerability management

- Define severity levels and remediation targets
- Track vulnerabilities in a shared system
- Review aging vulnerabilities regularly
- Prioritize fixes that reduce customer risk and attack surface

## Compliance readiness

For teams operating in regulated environments, clarify:

- Relevant standards and obligations
- Required controls and evidence expectations
- Audit support roles and timelines
- Control ownership and periodic verification

## Disaster recovery

Document and test recovery assumptions for critical services.

- Define recovery time objective (RTO)
- Define recovery point objective (RPO)
- Document failover and rollback procedures
- Test recovery periodically and track remediation actions

## Business continuity expectations

- Maintain continuity plans for mission-critical workflows
- Validate backup and restore procedures
- Ensure dependency maps are current
- Include disaster recovery learnings in roadmap prioritization

## Cost and sustainability

- Track service cost trends and unit economics where practical
- Set targets for efficiency and waste reduction
- Include cost impact in architectural decision records
